PC Building Simulator Lab

In this lab, you will learn the major components inside a desktop computer, review what each part does, and complete a drag-and-drop PC assembly activity. This lab supports hardware skills commonly introduced in IT support and CompTIA A+ preparation.

Learning Objectives

  • Identify the main hardware components inside a desktop computer.
  • Explain the purpose of the motherboard, CPU, RAM, storage, GPU, PSU, cooling, and case.
  • Practice placing components into the correct part of a PC build.
  • Use feedback to improve hardware troubleshooting and assembly knowledge.

Component Guide

Motherboard

The main circuit board that connects the CPU, RAM, storage, power, expansion cards, and ports.

CPU

The processor acts as the brain of the computer. It performs instructions and calculations.

RAM

Temporary memory used while programs are running. More RAM helps with multitasking.

Storage

HDDs and SSDs store the operating system, applications, and user files.

Power Supply

The PSU converts wall power into usable power for the computer components.

Graphics Card

The GPU processes graphics and video output. It is especially important for gaming and design work.

Cooling Fan

Cooling helps remove heat from the CPU, GPU, and case to prevent overheating.

Case

The case physically holds and protects the internal computer components.
